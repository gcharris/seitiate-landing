Effective Date: March 22, 2026

The Engine Posture: Seitiate is an open-source framework designed for terminal sovereignty. If you self-host the engine, all data processing occurs entirely within your own physical hardware or VPC. Agent-C LLC collects zero telemetry, zero creative content, and zero analytics from self-hosted Seitiate instances.

1. The Seitiate.com Website

The marketing and documentation website you are currently viewing (`seitiate.com`) collects zero personal information. We do not use cookies, trackers, or advertising pixels. Server logs operate on standard 30-day rolling deletion and do not identify users.

2. Data Liberation & The Vault

A core feature of the Seitiate framework is the Data Liberation Pipeline, which allows users to parse and import historical data exports (e.g., Google Takeout, Facebook archives) directly into the framework's knowledge base. This ingestion occurs strictly locally, and the resulting structure is stored exclusively inside the user's sovereign boundaries. Agent-C LLC receives no notification of, nor access to, imported data.

3. Framework Security Mechanisms

When running Seitiate (or a product built upon its SDK), the framework enforces strict cryptographic thresholds to protect your accumulated self-knowledge.

3.1 End-to-End Encryption and User-Held Keys

Your Memory (Codex), Canon calibration data, and Crystallization outputs are encrypted at the engine layer using keys that you hold. This ensures:

  • We cannot read your Codex. Your accumulated self-knowledge is encrypted with keys derived locally. Even if hosted on an enterprise endpoint like Sakme, the servers only store ciphertext.
  • You control access. Only you (or someone with your encryption keys) can decrypt your Memory. Agent-C LLC cannot access, search, or analyze your Codex contents under any circumstances.
  • Your keys, your data. If you lose your encryption keys and have not set up a local recovery method, your encrypted data becomes permanently inaccessible.

3.2 Provenance and Blockchain Records

The Seitiate engine includes modules to publish Talents or creative bundles to decentralize marketplaces with attached provenance certificates anchored to a blockchain smart contract. These on-chain records contain:

  • Cryptographic hashes of your published work (not the work itself).
  • Provenance scores measuring the degree of human creative governance.
  • Timestamps establishing absolute chronology.
  • Soulbound identity tokens linking the provenance to your creator identity.

On-chain records are immutable. However, these records contain no readable creative content. They are cryptographic artifacts entirely dependent on the decryption keys stored inside your encrypted Vault. If you wipe your Seitiate installation and destroy your keys, the on-chain records remain but become permanently inaccessible, effectively rendering them anonymous hash data detached from your identity.

3.3 Network Silence Sandbox

Agentic workflows executing within Seitiate's Copy-Mount Sandbox are rigidly sealed off from external networks via `network: none` docker configurations. AI generation loops cannot exfiltrate their internal working context.
