How to contribute to the open-source engine, the AGPL-3.0 rules for commercialization, and the one loophole regarding data sovereignty.

How can I contribute to Seitiate?

We actively welcome builders, developers, and researchers to get involved in shaping the Context Engine. As an open-source framework, Seitiate relies on aggressive peer review and community development to harden its security sandbox and governance modules.

The absolute best place to discuss architecture, propose pull requests, or get the framework running locally is in our developer Discord.

What does the AGPL-3.0 license restrict?

Seitiate is licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0). This guarantees that the open-source features will remain permanently open-source.

Crucially, it means the core tools and infrastructure cannot be taken private for direct commercial use or resold as proprietary software without distributing the source code. The framework is designed to be self-hosted by users, or modified publicly by contributors.

Can I build a commercial product on top of Seitiate?

Yes, but with an extreme constraint. Projects (like Sakme) can be built on top of the open-source infrastructure, and an enterprise/commercial license could be granted, provided one major rule is followed:

NO DATA HARVESTING. No commercial licenses will ever be granted to any company that intends to exploit, train on, or monetize downstream customer data.

The explicit exception to this rule is if the commercial project is built specifically to help the end-user monetize their own data. Suppose you want to commercialize a platform that helps users sell their own intelligence context; this is permitted only if it is fully transparent, and the ownership, specific permissions, and financial value of the data remain entirely with the person who generates it.
